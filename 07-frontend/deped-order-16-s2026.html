DepEd Order 16 s.2026: New Lesson Planning Guidelines Explained

DepEd Order 16 s.2026 introduced the ILAW lesson plan format as the new standard for all public school teachers under the MATATAG curriculum. This order replaces previous DLL/DLP requirements and shifts the focus from procedural documentation to student learning outcomes.

What Is DepEd Order 16 s.2026?

DepEd Order 16, Series of 2026, is the official policy that mandates the use of the ILAW lesson plan format across Philippine public schools. ILAW stands for Intentions, Learning Experiences, Assessment, and Ways Forward. The order is part of the broader MATATAG curriculum reform, which aims to reduce teacher administrative burden while improving learning quality.

What Does DO 16 s.2026 Change?

The order introduces several important changes:

  • Format change: The ILAW format replaces the traditional DLL and DLP formats.
  • Outcome focus: Teachers write clear learning intentions instead of minute-by-minute procedures.
  • Reduced paperwork: The format is designed to be shorter and more meaningful.
  • Alignment with MATATAG: All lesson plans must connect to MATATAG Curriculum Guide competencies.

Timeline for ILAW Implementation

The order was released in 2026 with phased implementation across regions. Some divisions began pilot implementation earlier, while others are rolling out during the school year. Check with your Schools Division Office (SDO) for your specific schedule and training dates.

How to Comply With DO 16 s.2026

  1. Use the ILAW format for all lesson plans.
  2. Base your Intentions on MATATAG Curriculum Guide competencies.
  3. Design Learning Experiences that actively engage students.
  4. Include both formative and summative Assessment.
  5. Plan Ways Forward for remediation and enrichment.

Common ILAW Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake 1: Mixing up Intentions with Learning Experiences. Intentions describe what students will learn; Learning Experiences describe how they will learn it.

Mistake 2: Writing objectives that are not measurable. Use action verbs like identify, explain, solve, create, and compare.

Mistake 3: Forgetting the Ways Forward section. Always plan next steps for both struggling and advanced learners.
