How to Make an ILAW Lesson Plan: Step-by-Step Guide

A practical guide for DepEd teachers transitioning to the ILAW format

Creating an ILAW lesson plan for the first time can feel overwhelming, especially if you are used to the old DLL or DLP format. This guide breaks it down into simple, actionable steps that any DepEd teacher can follow.

Step 1: Understand the ILAW Structure

The ILAW format has four sections: Intentions (what students will learn), Learning Experiences (how they will learn it), Assessment (how you will check understanding), and Ways Forward (what comes next). Before writing, make sure you understand what goes into each section.

Intentions — What Students Will Learn

State the learning competency from the MATATAG Curriculum Guide and write 2-3 measurable objectives. Example: "By the end of the lesson, students will be able to identify the main idea of a grade-level text." Use action verbs like identify, explain, solve, create, and compare.

Learning Experiences — How They'll Learn It

Describe the teaching activities. Include a variety of approaches: direct instruction, group work, independent practice, and hands-on activities. Reference design principles like scaffolding and checks for understanding.

Assessment — How You'll Check Understanding

Include formative checks (questions, exit tickets, observation) and summative assessment (quiz, performance task). Make sure your assessment matches your objectives.

Ways Forward — What Comes Next

Plan remediation for struggling students and enrichment for advanced learners. Connect to the next lesson so learning continues.

Step 2: Reference Your MATATAG CG

Your Learning Competency code (e.g., EN4LC-Ia-1.1) comes from the MATATAG Curriculum Guide. Make sure you have the correct CG for your grade level and subject. The competency is the foundation of your Intentions section.

Step 3: Write Each Section

Start with Intentions, then design Learning Experiences that lead to those outcomes, add Assessment that measures progress, and finish with Ways Forward. Keep your language clear and focused on student learning.

Step 4: Review Against DO 16 s.2026

Before submitting, check that your plan follows the ILAW structure, uses MATATAG competencies, and includes all four sections. A quick review can save you from revisions later.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I start writing an ILAW lesson plan?

Begin with the Learning Competency from the MATATAG Curriculum Guide, then write your Intentions, Learning Experiences, Assessment, and Ways Forward.

What goes in the Intentions section?

State the learning competency and 2-3 measurable objectives describing what students will learn.

What are examples of Learning Experiences?

Direct instruction, group work, hands-on activities, discussions, and independent practice.

How do I assess in ILAW format?

Use formative checks like questions and exit tickets, plus summative tasks like quizzes or performance tasks.

Can the generator write my ILAW plan for me?
