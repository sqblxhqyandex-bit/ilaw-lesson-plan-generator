ILAW Lesson Plan for Grade 4 — MATATAG Curriculum Aligned

Generate complete ILAW-format lesson plans for Grade 4 under the DepEd MATATAG K-10 curriculum. Grade 4 is a pivotal year where students transition from foundational skills to deeper conceptual understanding across all subjects.

Grade 4 Subjects Covered

  • English: Reading comprehension, writing narratives, grammar, vocabulary, oral communication, and literature appreciation.
  • Filipino: Pagbasa at pag-unawa, pagsulat ng talata, gramatika, talasalitaan, panitikang Pilipino, at pagsasalita.
  • Mathematics: Multi-digit operations, fractions, decimals, geometry, data interpretation, and problem solving.
  • Science: Matter, energy, ecosystems, Earth systems, scientific inquiry, weather and climate, and forces.
  • MAPEH: Music appreciation, visual arts techniques, team sports, health and nutrition, and physical fitness.
  • Araling Panlipunan: Philippine geography, pre-colonial history, local government, cultural communities, and map reading.
  • ESP: Values integration, social responsibility, environmental stewardship, respect for diversity, and personal growth.
  • EPP/TLE: Basic entrepreneurship, home economics, ICT literacy, agriculture basics, and livelihood skills.

Grade 4 Learning Characteristics

Grade 4 learners are typically 9-10 years old. They are developing greater independence and abstract thinking abilities. Key characteristics include:

  • Can handle longer periods of focused work (20-30 minutes per activity)
  • Developing logical reasoning and problem-solving skills
  • Increasingly capable of collaborative group work with defined roles
  • Enjoy hands-on experiments and project-based learning
  • Beginning to connect classroom learning to real-world contexts
  • Need clear rubrics and expectations for assessment

Grade 4 Math — Multiplication of Two-Digit Numbers

  • I: Students will multiply two-digit numbers by one-digit numbers.
  • L: Concrete models, guided practice, and problem-solving pairs.

Teaching Tips for Grade 4

Use graphic organizers to help students structure their thinking. Incorporate inquiry-based learning. Provide choice in assignments to increase engagement. Use formative assessment checkpoints throughout the lesson. Connect lessons to Filipino culture and local community contexts.
